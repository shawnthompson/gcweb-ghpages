Confirmation of previous Public Service Commission test results

  1. If you wrote a Public Service Commission (PSC) test more than two months ago but did not retain a copy of your results, fill out all required fields and submit the e-form below.
  2. If you completed a PSC test in a recent appointment process (within the last two months), the organization responsible for the process will send you a copy of your results.

Confirmation of previous PSC test results can only be provided for PSC exams. Allow a minimum of five business days' turnaround time upon receipt of your completed form. Be aware that hiring organizations sometimes request that you provide your results in a shorter period of time. If this is the case, you should ask the hiring organization to provide you with additional time.

Note that if you don't have a Personal Record Identifier (PRI), it is recommended that you insert your date of birth (full or partial); this will help correctly identify you and accelerate the process. Please do not send your Social Insurance Number, as it is impossible to conduct a search with it.

By submitting your application, you consent to have your previous PSC test results e-mailed to you, therefore you are responsible for providing a valid e-mail address. If you do not wish to receive your results by e-mail, please contact us at:

CFP.Resultats-Test-Results.PSC@cfp-psc.gc.ca
Phone:

Privacy Notice Statement

Privacy Notice Statement

Collection and use of personal information is in accordance with the Privacy Act. This personal information is used solely for the purposes of confirming that individual test results to be sent from the PSC's test results database to the requester do indeed belong to those of the requester. The personal information collected is held in Personal Information Bank “Second Language Evaluation (SLE) Test Results” and Personal Information Bank “Occupational Test Results” which are administered by the Personnel Psychology Centre of the Public Service Commission of Canada. This information is collected in accordance with paragraph 30(2) (a) and Section 11 of the PSEA. In some cases, information may be disclosed without your consent for purposes not outlined here pursuant to Subsection 8(2) of the Privacy Act. Hard copy files are retained for five (5) years and then destroyed. Candidate test results are kept indefinitely on computerized files.

The Privacy Act states that you have the right to access your personal information and to request changes to incorrect information. For further information, please contact the Personnel Psychology Centre at CFP.CPP-PPC.PSC@cfp-psc.gc.ca, or 819-420-6681. For more information about this Privacy Notice Statement, please contact our Privacy Coordinator.
